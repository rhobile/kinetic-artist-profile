Andrew Jones Kinetic Sculpture

Moved by the wind

I am moved emotionally by the wind: not so much the strong or steady winds like the Mistral or Bora but more gentle and uncertain air movement around gardens or architecture. Wind will be obvious if it is doing useful work driving something or showing its destructive power, but a wind stirred or stilled in a semi-sheltered like a garden has its own subtle and unexpected qualities. But of course, we will be unaware of any of this unless there is something to see moving.
These unexpected qualities are reflected in the sculptures which, when still, suggest rigidity rather than fluidity, but which are then transformed or altered in some way by their movement under the influence of moving air.
The structures I make are often very simple (like the Trio on the wall or Cube in Frame) and I do not wish them to suggest fluidity in themselves (while static), but then to reveal something unexpected or ambiguous and as unpredictable as the wind itself.
I use light alloys, fine stainless bearings and accurate balancing to ensure that the elements of each work can move in very light wind. Often, I use white paint: it is striking against dark foliage and quickly draws the eye to the movement when it happens. Some work uses small magnets to bring the linear elements to order when the air is still. Because the constructions are mainly slim, they will withstand quite strong wind (like bare branches in winter). However, I think they are at their best in a semi-sheltered area where the wind is having an argument or a gentle discussion with its surroundings.

In nature

I delight to find examples of unusual movement in nature like a rotating leaf suspended on spider silk and I hope that the presence of the sculpture will add to an appreciation of how trees and plants move and are seldom completely still.

The origins

While studying art and product design at Newcastle in 1972, fellow student Ray Merry and I experimented with various materials and structures observing their movement in the wind. This was an art project rather than product design, but led, almost by accident, to the development of a power kite which we subsequently patented. This aerofoil kite is controllable, hard pulling and in windy weather can give you quite a work-out. It became the first product of The Flexifoil Kite Company. For over 30 years I was involved in the development of this and other power kites all with the express purpose of pulling hard under maximum control. I still enjoy the thrill of powerful kites, but I am returning to those experimental roots of 1972, to explore the wind for its own sake. br>


Work

I have displayed my work in many exhibitions in East Anglia UK including a regular July exhibition in our garden in Ely for Cambridge Open Studios. For many years I have been delighted to take on commissions for private gardens (either wall mounted or post mounted) in the UK, Australia, New Zealand and the United States.
