Andrew Jones Kinetic Sculpture

Moved by the wind

I am moved emotionally by the wind: not so much the strong or steady winds like the Mistral or Bora but more gentle and uncertain air movement around gardens or architecture. Wind forces are chaotic and unpredictable.

These unexpected qualities are reflected in the sculptures which, when still, suggest rigidity rather than fluidity, but which are then transformed or altered in some way by their movement under the influence of the air.

The structures I make are often very simple (like the Trio on the wall or Cube in Frame) and I do not wish them to suggest fluidity in themselves (while static), but then to reveal something unexpected when moving.

I use light alloys, fine stainless bearings and accurate balancing to ensure that the elements of each work can move in very light wind. Some work uses small magnets to bring the linear elements to rest in predictable alignments.

In nature

I delight to find examples of unusual movement in nature like a rotating leaf suspended on spider silk and I hope that the presence of the sculpture will add to an appreciation of how trees and plants interact with the air.

The origins

While studying art and product design at Newcastle in 1972, fellow student Ray Merry and I experimented with various materials and structures observing their movement in the wind. This was an art project that evolved into a lifelong pursuit.
